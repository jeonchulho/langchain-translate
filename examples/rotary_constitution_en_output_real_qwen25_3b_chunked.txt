World Congress Article

- Article 1 - Period and Location. The RI World Congress is held at the location and time determined by the Board of Directors during the final three months of the Rotary Year.
- Article 2 - Special World Congress. In case of a special emergency, the President may call for a Special World Congress with approval from the Board of Directors.
- Article 3 - Delegates and Voting. The election committee consists of delegates holding credentials and proxies appointed by clubs and Extraordinary Members.

- (a) Clubs have the right to cast votes through one representative or proxy per club member, if they hold more than one vote. Clubs with over 50 members can exercise voting rights proportional to half their excess membership.
- (b) RI Officers and former RI Presidents are automatically Extraordinary Members of the World Congress.

Article 4 - Voting. Voting is carried out in accordance with the Rules of RI.

Rules Committee Article

- Article 1 - Purpose. The Rules Committee serves as the legislative body of RI.
- Article 2 - Period and Location. The Rules Committee meets annually in April, May, or June, with preference given to April. The date and location are determined by the Board of Directors. If the Board does not convene a local meeting due to insufficient attendance (less than two-thirds) of directors for financial or other reasons, it will be held at an adjacent region near RI headquarters.
- Article 3 - Procedure. The Rules Committee reviews proposed rules that have been formally introduced. The decisions of the Rules Committee must be approved by each club's Board.

Extraordinary Members' qualifications are determined by the Bylaws.

Article 4 - Interim Meetings. The Board of Directors may convene an interim meeting of the Rules Committee with a vote of 90% of attending directors. The date and location for this meeting is decided by the Board of Directors, and only proposed legislation will be considered. Time limitations and procedures in RI Bylaws do not apply unless otherwise specified.

Decisions made at an interim meeting are ratified by each club as stipulated in Article 3.

Article 5 - Membership Dues

Each club and Rotary Club must pay dues to RI twice a year, either with one member per club paying or according to the schedule set by the Board of Directors.

Foundation Article

- Article 1 - Establish and operate the RI Foundation in accordance with the Bylaws of RI.
- Article 2 - All donations received by RI, including gifts and bequests, as well as income derived from these donations, are managed by the RI Foundation.

The surplus of approved RI is all part of the foundation's assets.

Article 12: Membership Designations and Titles

- Article 1: Regular Members. Regular members are called Rotaryists, and they can wear the RI flag, badge, and other insignia.
- Article 2: Honorary Members. Honorary members are called honorary Rotarians, and they can also wear the RI flag, badge, and other insignia.
- Article 3: Rotary Club Members. Each member of a Rotary club is called a Rotary Club Member, and they can wear the Rotary Club's flag, badge, and other insignia.

Article 13: Bylaws

The Board of Directors may adopt or amend bylaws that add additional provisions not in conflict with this constitution to manage RI affairs.

Article 14: Interpretation

In this constitution and RI bylaws, 'shall', 'is', and 'are' indicate mandatory actions, while 'may' and 'should' indicate permissible actions. The terms "mail", "send", "postal vote", and "club vote" also include the use of electronic mail (email) and internet technology for cost savings and to improve response rates.

Article 15: Amendments

RI bylaws can only be amended with a two-thirds majority vote at the Board of Directors meeting.
